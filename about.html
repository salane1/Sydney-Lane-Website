About

Sydney Lane is a long-time audiophile and pandemic-turned composer and multi-instrumentalist. She deems childhood
mother-daughter symphony orchestra dates and her father's Dropkick Murphys school morning car ride music as the
source of her creativity and initiation of what will be a life full of music.

During the Covid-19 pandemic, Sydney found a new energy towards music creation, released a compilation of songs for
film on SoundCloud, and realized just how healing music can be. Neoclassical composers, like Philip Glass and Max
Richter, were inspirations for this new energy.

Sydney wanted to continue playing as much as she was during the pandemic, after the pandemic. To avoid making music
her post-pandemic hobby, she transferred from St. Edward's in Austin, to Loyola University in New Orleans ('23) in hopes
of a career in the entertainment industry (i.e., music for film). At Loyola, she is studying Music Business and has taken
classes encompassing copyright law, digital audio production, artist and entertainment finance, popular music history,
and film scoring.
